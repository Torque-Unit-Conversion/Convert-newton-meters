The Complete Beginner’s Guide to Torque Unit Conversion

Torque is everywhere. It appears in car repair manuals, bicycle assembly guides, industrial machinery specs, and engineering textbooks. Yet many beginners struggle with one simple issue: converting torque from one unit to another.

If you’ve ever wondered how to convert Newton meters (Nm) to foot-pounds (ft-lb) correctly, this guide will walk you through everything step by step — in plain language.

What Is Torque?

Torque is a measure of rotational force. In simple terms, it tells you how much force is applied to turn or rotate an object around an axis.

Torque is calculated as:

Torque = Force × Distance

If you apply force at a distance from the pivot point, you create rotational motion.

Why Are There Different Torque Units?

Different measurement systems use different torque units.

Metric System

  • Newton meters (Nm)

Imperial System

  • Foot-pounds (ft-lb)
  • Inch-pounds (in-lb)

Because tools, manuals, and machines may use either system, understanding torque conversion is essential.

What Is a Newton Meter (Nm)?

A Newton meter represents the torque produced when a force of one Newton is applied at a distance of one meter from the pivot point.

What Is a Foot-Pound (ft-lb)?

A foot-pound represents the torque created when one pound of force is applied one foot away from the pivot point.

The Exact Conversion Formula: Nm to ft-lb

1 Newton meter = 0.737562 foot-pounds

To convert Nm to ft-lb:

ft-lb = Nm × 0.737562

To convert ft-lb to Nm:

Nm = ft-lb × 1.35582

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 1 Nm exactly 0.74 ft-lb?

No. 1 Nm equals 0.737562 ft-lb. 0.74 is a rounded approximation.

Can over-tightening cause damage?

Yes. Over-tightening can strip threads, crack components, and deform metal parts.

Why do some tools show lb-ft instead of ft-lb?

Both are commonly used for torque notation, though lb-ft is technically more precise.
